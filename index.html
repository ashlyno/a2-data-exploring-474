Assignment 2: Bird Plane Incident

By Ashlyn Opgrande

Bird and Plane Collisions in the Northwest

Visual encodings I used here include coloring and position. In order to not make such a large amount of data look increasingly overwhelming, I wanted to avoid using too much visual encoding. Coloring here is used to show each type of data shown by the selected feature. So each circle is either colored by species, airport, or specific airline. I chose to do this so users can begin to identify patterns in the dataset. Such as, if a particular airport or species seems to have more of the incidents than others. Then position is used to show where the incidents occur based on the planes speed and height. This helps us see that most incidents occur when the plane is likely on the runway rather than in the air.

Control features I used here include coloring (for species, airport, or airline), year filtering, and time of day filtering. I chose the control feature for coloring to allow users to quickly search for trends in the data by either species, airports, or airlines. However, there is a lot of data being shown here, so to get more specific data users can continue to filter the data down by year or time of day. I thought all of this categorical data was essential to analysis because it does reveal some interesting data. Such as certain years have more entries and time of day seems to be a major factor in the amount of incidents that occur.
